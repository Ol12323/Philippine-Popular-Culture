Games

Basketball

Basketball is a popular sport in the country. Everyone knows how to play it—from toddlers to adults, and even the elderly. Their passion for the game borders on obsession. It's not unusual to see makeshift courts or hoops in vacant lots until government officials removed them for safety purposes. The country's PBA (Philippine Basketball Association) is the first professional basketball league in Asia and the second oldest in the world next to NBA; so yeah, people love this game.

Boxing

Boxing is a popular sport in the Philippines. If there is one person who could clear road traffic in this country, it is Manny “Pacman” Pacquiao. Everyone watches his fights on TV or radios, and many Filipinos are big fans of his. The Philippines has produced some of the world's best boxers, including Ceferino Garcia and Nonito Donaire; these are just two examples of how important a role boxing plays in Filipino culture.

Volleyball

Volleyball is a popular sport that is often played in backyards, vacant lots, and in beaches, too. Courts made of a volleyball net attached to bamboo poles with the bottom part of the bamboo buried on the ground are common sights. While it is played everywhere by all genders, professional volleyball is a female-dominated team sport in the Philippines.
