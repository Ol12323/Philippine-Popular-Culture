Arts

Brief Introduction

The traditional Filipino and Spanish Catholic traditions are mixed with elements from other parts of Asia and America to create the culture of the Philippines. Filipinos value their families, are frequently religious, and appreciate the appreciation of art, fashion, music, and food.

Music

Music in the Philippines originate and categorize as indigenous music which called as “Gong” and Hispanic-influenced music like rondalla , harana and kundiman, tinikling, and Cariñosa. In addition, there are Original Pilipino Music, Original Pinoy Music, and Original Philippine Music collectively go by the initials "OPM" for Filipino pop. One example of Filipino pride is the boyband SB19, which popularized OPM (Pinoy Music) abroad.

Dance

The Maria Clara Dance, Cordillera Dance, Muslim Dance, Rural Dance, and Tribal Dance are the five different types of folk dances in the Philippines. The most well-known type of Filipino dance, these folk dances from the Philippines express the fiesta spirit and enthusiasm for life.

Traditional Arts

Painting

Oil Painting

One method of painting that Filipino use is oil painting. This method was introduced as early as the 16th century and was influenced by European traditions. In the 19th century, the Philippines deviated from religious motifs and turned to more secular art.

Soil Painting

Waway Saway soil painting works

As many artist use watercolors to paint, the Talaanding, an indigenous people of the Banwaon tribe use soil for painting. This method of painting was introduced by Waway Saway in the Talaanding community in the year of 1995. The picture depicts their tribe’s life, beliefs, practices, and close association with the land.

Pottery

Neolithic Pottery

During the Neolithic period, Philippine pottery was used to suit individual household needs. It shows an example of a cultural link between the archaeological past and the ethnographic present. With its unique attributes, The Manunngual Jar put on the 100 PHP bill in the year of 1995.

Palayok

Anthropomorphic Pots

The jars are anthropomorphic; characterized by a design that suggests human figures with complete or partial facial features of the first inhabitants of Mindanao. Furthermore, they give emphasis on the Filipinos’ popular belief in life after death.

Weaving

Banig

The weaving process consists of five basic operations, shedding, picking, beating up, leaving off, and taking up. Banig weaving is a genuine treasure handed down as a tradition or a trade from one generation to another as it is widely practiced in the country. Its ingenuity is very much employed in its creation and the designs may vary from the practices of those from other regions

Bayong

Bayong refers to bags from the Philippines made by weaving dried leaves traditionally from Buri in Visayas and Pandan in Luzon.
