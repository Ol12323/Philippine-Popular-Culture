Abaca basket

Abaca is a kind of banana that is produced commercially in the Philippines. Abaca from the Philipines is considered the world’s strongest natural fiber. Abaca fibers may also be used to make a wide range of handcrafted items, including clothing, textiles, sporting goods, packaging materials, and ornaments. One of the local products in the Philippines is the Abaca basket, which may be used for a variety purposes such as planter, a small hamper, a decorative item, and much more. Abaca, a local natural commodity, has long been employed in Bicol’s customary planting methods.

Walis Tambo

Walis Tambo is a Philippine cultural icon. The Tambo is much more than a broom. It is a component of Filipino’s daily lives. It results in a gleaming and clean floor. It is convenient and may be used anywhere in the house (but not the bathroom as a mop or a walis tingting might be more useful). this soft bristles are created from the phragmites reed known as tambo in the Philippines, thus the name of walis tambo.

Banig

According to the website of the National Commission on Culture and the Arts(NCCA), banig is a traditional handwoven mat created in the Philippines. It is not technically, a textile. Furthermore, the mat is composed of buri(palm), pandan or seagrass leaves, or native leaves, depending on the area in the Philippines.

Abaniko

The Abaniko is a Filipino cultural and traditional item meant for everybody usage. With its traditional woven design, this hand-held fan will also keep you cool in style. Its’s ideal for summer, but you can also use it if there’s a brownout in your home. It it available in market palces

Walis Tingting

The broomstick has been long a part of Filipino life, with walis tingting being used by yard sweeping. Its’s a broom constructed from thin palm leaf midribs. On one end, the rigid ribs are tied. It is typically used in conjunction with a simple dustpan. Walis tingting is a broomstick fashioned form coconut midribs in Tagalog. The words “walis” and “tingting” refer to the midribs of the coconut leaves used to make the broomstick.
